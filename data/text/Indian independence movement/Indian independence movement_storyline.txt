# Indian independence movement: Comic Storyline

## Overview
The Indian independence movement was a decades-long struggle against British colonial rule, led by various leaders and movements, ultimately resulting in the country's independence in 1947. The movement was marked by key events, such as the Jallianwala Bagh massacre and the non-violent resistance of Mahatma Gandhi.

## Main Characters
- **Mahatma Gandhi**: A key leader of the Indian independence movement, known for his philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience.
- **Jawaharlal Nehru**: A prominent leader of the Indian National Congress and a key figure in the country's struggle for independence.
- **Veerapandiya Kattabomman**: An 18th-century Polygar and chieftain from Tamil Nadu who waged war against the East India Company.
- **Birsa Munda**: A leader of the Munda community who led a rebellion against British rule in Eastern India.
- **Rani Velu Nachiyar**: A queen of Sivaganga who formed an army and challenged the British in battle.

## Act 1: Early Resistance (1750s-1850s)

The Indian independence movement began with early resistance to British rule, led by figures such as Maveeran Alagumuthu Kone, Puli Thevar, and Maruthanayagam Pillai. These leaders fought against the British and their allies, but were ultimately defeated. The movement gained momentum in the 19th century, with the Santhal Hul and the Kol Insurrection, led by Sidhu, Kanhu, and Birsa Munda. These uprisings were marked by violence and brutality, with the British using force to suppress the rebellions.

## Act 2: The Rebellion of 1857 (1857)

The Indian Rebellion of 1857 was a major uprising against British rule, sparked by the use of cow and pig fat in rifle cartridges. The rebellion was led by sepoys and other Indian soldiers who were dissatisfied with their treatment by the British. The rebellion was marked by violence and brutality, with the British using force to suppress the uprising.

## Act 3: The Non-Violent Resistance (1915-1947)

The Indian independence movement took a new turn with the non-violent resistance of Mahatma Gandhi. Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence and civil disobedience inspired a new generation of leaders, including Jawaharlal Nehru and Vallabhbhai Patel. The movement was marked by key events, such as the Salt March and the Quit India Movement.
